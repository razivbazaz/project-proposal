Concept & Implementation Proposal

1. Summary of Your Vision

The goal is to create a digital platform that highlights local and niche European brands—starting with cosmetics in France—and builds trust through deep editorial content and controlled product exposure.

The platform will:

  • Showcase local European brands, especially cosmetics and lifestyle labels.
  • Publish editorial case studies, reviews, interviews, and brand stories.
  • Build consumer trust through curated, high-quality content.
  • Offer vetted brands the option to feature products, only after approval and agreement.
  • Evolve into a controlled marketplace, where brands can list products under clear rules and quality standards.

2. Proposed Platform Structure

A. Homepage (Editorial-Led)

  • Hero section explaining the mission and value of the platform.
  • “Featured Brands of the Month” area to highlight selected partners.
  • Spotlight on latest case studies, interviews, and editorial pieces.
  • Search and filters by Brand / Country / Category.

B. Brand Landing Pages (One Page per Brand)

  • Brand profile: story, origin, mission, positioning.
  • Editorial case study or interview (produced by you or your editorial team).
  • Image gallery (brand visuals, product shots, behind-the-scenes).
  • Area for key products, to be expanded when product listing is enabled.
  • Links to brand website or “Where to Buy” calls-to-action.

C. Category Pages

  • Cosmetics & Skincare
  • Fragrance
  • Wellness & Self-care
  • Home & Lifestyle
  • Eco-friendly / Organic brands
  • Local French brands & regional highlights

D. Editorial / Blog Section

  • In-depth product reviews and ingredient breakdowns.
  • Trend analysis within cosmetics and lifestyle sectors.
  • Founder interviews and behind-the-brand stories.
  • Factory visits or process-focused pieces (leveraging your 6+ years in cosmetics).

E. Brand Application Page

Dedicated page where brands can apply to be featured, including:

  • Brand & contact details, product categories, links to website and social media.
  • Certification and positioning (organic, cruelty-free, local, artisanal, etc.).
  • Options for: free editorial consideration, sponsored features, or product listing in the future.

F. Brand Dashboard (Phase 2)

In a later phase, approved brands can:

  • Log in and propose product listings.
  • Upload product images and key details for editorial review.
  • Request premium placements or additional promotional packages.

3. Unique Selling Proposition (USP)

A. Trust-Building Editorial Model

Instead of jumping directly to a large marketplace, the platform puts storytelling and reviews first. This is particularly effective in cosmetics and lifestyle segments, where credibility and transparency matter.

B. Focus on French & European Niche Brands

The platform targets boutique labels, artisan producers, and sustainable brands—not global mass-market giants. This gives a clear identity: a curated guide to quality European brands.

C. Industry Experience as a Differentiator

Your six years of experience working with cosmetic plants in France is a strong trust signal. It supports a more technical and knowledgeable editorial voice, different from generic review blogs.

D. Controlled Marketplace (Future)

Only vetted, approved brands can list products, preventing low-quality or fake items. The platform remains curated, not open to uncontrolled mass listings.

4. Revenue Model Options

Phase 1 – Editorial / Awareness Stage

  • Sponsored brand pages or featured placements.
  • Sponsored product reviews and special editorial formats.
  • Advertising placements (display ads or brand partnerships).
  • Affiliate revenue via links to brand shops or retailers.

Phase 2 – Monetising Brand Pages

  • Brand page setup fee (including editorial production).
  • Optional recurring subscription for maintaining and promoting the listing.
  • Premium placements on homepage, category pages, and newsletters.

Phase 3 – Marketplace Model

  • Commission on each sale generated through the platform.
  • Monthly store fee or listing fee for brands that actively sell.
  • Potential logistics/fulfilment partnerships if you decide to handle stock later.

5. Project Roadmap

Phase 1 – Discovery & Design (Approx. 2–3 Weeks)

  • Confirm brand segments and content priorities.
  • Create wireframes for homepage, brand pages, and editorial layouts.
  • Define editorial style, tone of voice, and visual direction.

Phase 2 – Development (Approx. 4–6 Weeks)

  • Implement the site on a suitable CMS (e.g., WordPress with a custom theme).
  • Set up templates for brand profiles, category pages, and blog posts.
  • Integrate brand application form and basic moderation workflow.
  • Implement SEO structure (URLs, metadata, schema, internal linking).

Phase 3 – Initial Content Creation (Ongoing)

  • Produce the first batch of brand profiles and case studies (e.g. 5–10 brands).
  • Create supporting editorial pieces (trend, ingredient analysis, etc.).
  • Prepare visual content: photography, graphics, and brand-specific visuals.

Phase 4 – Launch & Growth

  • Soft launch with a first set of French cosmetic brands.
  • Outreach to additional local brands and partners across Europe.
  • Promote via social media, newsletters, and industry contacts.
  • Measure traffic, engagement, and brand interest; refine packages.

Phase 5 – Expansion (Optional Extensions)

  • Add brand dashboards for product submission and collaboration.
  • Enable curated product listings and, later, transactional ecommerce flows.
  • Introduce video interviews, webinars, and possibly live brand events.

6. What We Need to Begin

  • Shortlist of initial brands (3–5) to feature at launch.
  • Logo, colour palette, and basic branding preferences.
  • Decision on language scope (French only, or French + English).
  • Any existing material: past case studies, testimonials, or brand contacts.
  • Clarification of Year 1 goals: visibility, number of brands, revenue targets.

7. Our Recommendation

Start content-first, then evolve into a curated marketplace.
We recommend beginning with strong editorial positioning—showcasing your expertise and providing real value to both brands and consumers. Once a base of trusted brands and regular visitors is established, we can gradually introduce paid placements and product listings under a controlled, high-quality model.
